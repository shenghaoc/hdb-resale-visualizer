# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary users are Singapore HDB resale buyers evaluating specific flats, blocks, towns, or shortlists before offering or negotiating. They care about comparable resale transactions, remaining lease risk, monthly affordability, nearby amenities, and whether a seller's asking price is defensible.

Secondary users include agents, family members, or advisors helping a buyer interpret the same evidence. All workflows remain buyer-first.

## Product Purpose

A map-first due-diligence tool that helps HDB resale buyers decide whether an asking price is fair by comparing it with historical resale transactions, lease constraints, affordability constraints, and local context. Success means a buyer walks into a negotiation with transparent, deterministic evidence — not generated forecasts or opaque scores.

This is not a price prediction product, not a seller listing portal, and not a marketplace. It exists to build buyer confidence through data.

## Brand Personality

**Trustworthy, precise, utilitarian.** The interface projects calm competence — like a well-organized spreadsheet elevated into a spatial tool. Every number has a provenance; every label is concrete. Voice is professional and direct, never marketing-speak. Reference: Stripe Dashboard for clarity and information architecture.

## Anti-references

- **PropertyGuru's cluttered listings** — no grid-of-thumbnails browsing, no "hot deals" banners, no promoted inventory
- **Zillow's consumer fluff** — no "dream home" language, no lifestyle imagery, no vague market sentiment scores
- **Flashy real-estate startup aesthetic** — no gradient hero sections, no animated stat counters, no oversized decorative illustrations

The product should feel like a professional tool, not a consumer marketplace.

## Design Principles

1. **Deterministic evidence, not predictions** — Every number traces back to official public datasets. No black-box scores, no AI-generated forecasts, no opaque "market confidence" metrics. Show the math.

2. **Map-first spatial understanding** — Location grounds every decision. Proximity to MRT, schools, and amenities is visual and spatial, not a text list. The map is not decoration; it is the primary lens.

3. **Professional density over decoration** — Buyers need compact, scan-friendly data tables and comparisons. High information density with tight headers, small uppercase metadata labels, and minimal chrome. No decorative dashboards that hide the evidence.

4. **Buyer confidence through transparency** — Surface comparable transactions, price bands, lease decay calculations, affordability signals, and negotiation context. Every insight must be traceable to its source data.

5. **Privacy by default** — Browser-local state is the baseline and works fully offline. Shortlist cloud sync is opt-in through an anonymous sync code with no account, email, password, or PII.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 compliance. The product serves a broad demographic including older buyers and family advisors. Key considerations: sufficient color contrast for data-dense views, keyboard-navigable filter and comparison workflows, and respect for reduced-motion preferences in map and chart transitions.
